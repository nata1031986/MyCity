Manyava Skete of the Exaltation of the Holy Cross

Manyava Skete of the Exaltation of the Holy Cross is an Orthodox monastery located in the village of Manyava, Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast, about 40 km southwest of Ivano-Frankivsk. The monastery was founded by the Orthodox saint Job of Manyava in 1611. In 1785, it was closed by the Austrian government as part of the religious reform of Emperor Joseph II.

Short history

Ivano-Frankivsk is a city in western Ukraine. It got its name from two famous Ukrainians, Ivan Franko and Yuriy Fedkovych. The city has a long history, starting in the 17th century. Over the years, it belonged to different countries and empires.
