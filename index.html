Places of Power

Manyava Skete, located in Ukraine's Carpathian Mountains, is a historic Orthodox monastery founded in 1611. Known as the "Ukrainian Athos," it's famed for its spiritual tranquility and ancient architecture, including the Church of the Exaltation of the Holy Cross. The monastery also features a holy well, believed to have healing properties.

Places of Power

Spanning about 3 hectares in size and reaching depths of up to 1.5 meters, Nesamovyte Lake is steeped in legend and folklore. One of the most popular legends tells of a giant who, upon being deceived by his bride, cried tears that filled the lake. Locals and visitors alike are captivated by such tales, adding a mystical aura to the lake's natural beauty.

Short history

Ivano-Frankivsk is a city in western Ukraine. It got its name from two famous Ukrainians, Ivan Franko and Yuriy Fedkovych. The city has a long history, starting in the 17th century. Over the years, it belonged to different countries and empires.
